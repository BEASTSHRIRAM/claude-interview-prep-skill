# HR Reference -- Behavioural and HR Round

## The STAR Framework

Use this structure for all behavioural questions.
- **Situation**: set the scene, give context
- **Task**: what was your responsibility or the challenge you faced
- **Action**: what specific steps did you take (focus on YOUR actions, not the team's)
- **Result**: what was the outcome, quantify if possible

## Most Common HR Questions

### Tell Me About Yourself
Structure: Present -> Past -> Future
"I am currently in my final year of BTech CSE at [College]. Over the past [X] years I have worked on [key projects or internships], which helped me build strong skills in [skills]. Before that, I [relevant background]. I am particularly excited about this role because [connect to the company/role]."

Do NOT: read your resume. Ramble. Start from childhood.

### Why Do You Want to Join This Company?
Research specifically: their product, tech stack, culture, recent news, team size.
"I have been following [Company] for some time. I use [their product] and was impressed by [specific thing]. The engineering blog post about [technical topic] showed me how thoughtful the engineering culture is here. I want to contribute to [specific team/problem] because it aligns with my interest in [area]."

### What Are Your Strengths?
Pick two or three strengths that are relevant to the role. Back each with a brief example.
Good strengths for CSE roles: problem solving, attention to detail, quick learner, collaborative, ownership mindset.

### What Is Your Weakness?
Pick a real but manageable weakness. Show self-awareness and improvement.
Structure: Name the weakness -> What you are doing to improve -> Evidence of progress.
"I sometimes spend too much time trying to make code perfect before shipping. I have been working on this by setting time-boxed sprints and reminding myself that iteration matters more than perfection at the early stage."

### Tell Me About a Challenge You Faced
Use STAR. Pick a genuine technical or collaborative challenge from a project or internship.
Show that you stayed calm, took initiative, and learned from it.

### Where Do You See Yourself in 5 Years?
Show ambition but be realistic. Connect it to what the company offers.
"In five years I see myself as a solid backend engineer who has owned end-to-end features and ideally started contributing to architectural decisions. I want to grow in a place where I can learn from experienced engineers, and [Company] seems like exactly that environment."

### Why Should We Hire You?
Briefly summarize: what you bring (skills + attitude), how it matches what they need, what makes you different.
Do NOT be arrogant. Do NOT be generic.

### Do You Have Any Questions for Us?
Always say yes. Ask at least two genuine questions.
- "What does the onboarding process look like for new engineers?"
- "What is the biggest technical challenge the team is currently working on?"
- "What does growth look like for someone in this role?"
- "How does the team handle code reviews and knowledge sharing?"

## Behavioural Question Bank

**Teamwork and Collaboration**
- Tell me about a time you worked in a team that disagreed. How did you handle it?
- Tell me about a time you had to help a teammate who was struggling.
- Describe a situation where you had to take a leadership role unexpectedly.

**Problem Solving**
- Tell me about the most technically challenging project you have worked on.
- Describe a time when your first solution did not work. What did you do?
- Tell me about a time you had to learn something new very quickly.

**Ownership and Initiative**
- Tell me about a time you went beyond what was expected.
- Describe a situation where you identified a problem before it was assigned to you.
- Tell me about a side project you worked on out of your own interest.

**Communication**
- Tell me about a time you had to explain a technical concept to a non-technical person.
- Describe a situation where a misunderstanding caused a problem and how you fixed it.

**Handling Failure**
- Tell me about a mistake you made. What did you learn?
- Describe a project that did not go as planned. What would you do differently?

**Pressure and Deadlines**
- Tell me about a time you had to meet a tight deadline. How did you manage it?
- How do you prioritize when you have multiple things to do at once?

## Resume Walkthrough Tips

When asked "Walk me through your resume":
- Do not read it line by line
- Highlight the two or three most relevant experiences for THIS role
- For each experience, mention: what you did, what you learned, what impact it had
- End with why you are here today

**For Projects**
Be ready to answer:
- What was the problem you were solving?
- What was your specific contribution?
- What technical decisions did you make and why?
- What would you do differently if you started over?
- What was the most challenging part?

## Salary Negotiation (for Offers)

- Do not be the first to name a number if possible
- Research the market rate (Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, LinkedIn Salary)
- If asked for expected CTC: give a range based on research, or say "I am flexible and want to be compensated fairly for the value I bring"
- Always negotiate the first offer professionally. A counteroffer is expected and normal.
- Consider the full package: base, variable pay, equity, benefits, learning opportunities

## Attitude Signals Interviewers Look For

- Curiosity: do you ask good questions?
- Ownership: do you say "I" or always "we"? Do you take accountability?
- Coachability: when pushed back on an answer, do you get defensive or genuinely reconsider?
- Communication: can you explain your thought process clearly under pressure?
- Culture fit: do you seem like someone the team would enjoy working with?
