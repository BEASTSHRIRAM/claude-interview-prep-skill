# Aptitude Reference -- Quantitative, Logical, and Verbal

## Quantitative Aptitude

### Number System
- Divisibility Rules: by 2 (last digit even), by 3 (sum of digits divisible by 3), by 9 (sum of digits divisible by 9), by 11 (alternating sum divisible by 11)
- LCM x HCF = Product of two numbers
- Sum of first n natural numbers = n(n+1)/2
- Sum of squares: n(n+1)(2n+1)/6
- Sum of cubes: [n(n+1)/2]^2

### Percentages
- X% of Y = Y% of X = XY/100
- Percentage change = (New minus Old) / Old x 100
- Successive percentage changes: A% followed by B% = A + B + AB/100

### Profit and Loss
- Profit = SP minus CP
- Loss = CP minus SP
- Profit% = Profit/CP x 100
- SP = CP x (100 + Profit%)/100
- If marked price is MP and discount is D%: SP = MP x (100 minus D)/100
- Dishonest shopkeeper using false weight: Gain% = (True weight minus False weight)/False weight x 100

### Simple and Compound Interest
- Simple Interest = PRT/100, Amount = P + SI
- Compound Interest: A = P(1 + R/100)^T, CI = A minus P
- When compounded half-yearly: A = P(1 + R/200)^(2T)
- Difference between CI and SI for 2 years = P(R/100)^2

### Time and Work
- If A does work in x days, rate = 1/x per day
- Combined rate = 1/x + 1/y
- If A is twice as efficient as B: A takes half the time B takes
- Work equivalence: M1 x D1 x H1 = M2 x D2 x H2

### Speed, Distance, Time
- Speed = Distance/Time
- Average speed for equal distances = 2xy/(x+y) where x and y are speeds
- If speeds are in ratio a:b, times are in ratio b:a for same distance
- Relative speed: same direction = |S1 minus S2|, opposite direction = S1 + S2
- Train problems: time = (Length of train + Length of object) / Speed

### Ratio and Proportion
- a:b::c:d means ad = bc (product of extremes = product of means)
- If a:b = 3:4 and b:c = 5:6, then a:b:c = 15:20:24

### Probability
- P(A) = Favourable outcomes / Total outcomes
- P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) minus P(A and B)
- P(A and B) = P(A) x P(B) if independent
- P(not A) = 1 minus P(A)

### Permutation and Combination
- nPr = n! / (n-r)! -- arrangements matter
- nCr = n! / (r! x (n-r)!) -- arrangements do not matter
- nCr = nC(n-r)
- Circular permutations: (n-1)!
- Arrangements of n items with repetition: n^r

### Mixtures and Alligation
- Alligation rule: (Cheaper quantity)/(Dearer quantity) = (Mean price minus Cheaper price)/(Dearer price minus Mean price)

## Logical Reasoning

### Syllogisms
Draw Venn diagrams. Check if conclusion must be true, may be true, or cannot be true.

### Blood Relations
Map out the family tree. Common confusions: maternal vs paternal, in-laws.

### Seating Arrangements
Circular (fix one person, arrange rest in (n-1)!) vs Linear. Read constraints carefully, assign positions step by step.

### Series Completion
Look for arithmetic/geometric patterns, squares, cubes, prime numbers, alternating patterns, or differences.

### Coding-Decoding
Identify the shift pattern (alphabetical shift, reverse alphabet, positional coding).

### Direction Sense
Always draw the path. Remember compass directions. Clockwise and anticlockwise turns.

### Clocks
- Minute hand moves 6 degrees per minute, hour hand moves 0.5 degrees per minute
- Angle between hands = |30H minus 5.5M| where H is hours and M is minutes
- Hands coincide every 65 and 5/11 minutes
- Hands are at right angles 44 times in 24 hours

### Calendars
- Odd days: January=3, February=0(28 days), March=3, April=2, May=3, June=2, July=3, August=3, September=2, October=3, November=2, December=3
- Leap year: divisible by 4, but century year must be divisible by 400

## Verbal Ability

### Reading Comprehension
- Read the questions before the passage to know what to look for
- Main idea questions: look at first and last paragraphs
- Inference questions: do not go beyond what the passage states

### Sentence Completion
Look for contrast signals (however, but, although) or continuation signals (moreover, furthermore, similarly) to predict the missing word.

### Error Spotting
Common errors: subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, pronoun reference, misplaced modifiers, preposition usage.

### Vocabulary
Common GRE-level words frequently seen in aptitude tests: Pragmatic, Verbose, Laconic, Erudite, Pellucid, Ambiguous, Ephemeral, Tenacious.

## Company-Specific Aptitude Patterns

**TCS NQT**
- Numerical Ability: 26 questions, 40 minutes
- Verbal Ability: 24 questions, 30 minutes
- Reasoning Ability: 30 questions, 50 minutes
- Programming Logic (TCS specific): 10 questions, 15 minutes
- Coding: 1 to 2 problems, 45 minutes

**Infosys**
- Reasoning (25 questions, 35 minutes)
- Math (10 questions, 25 minutes)
- Verbal (40 questions, 35 minutes)
- Puzzle test: Sudoku, blood relation, seating arrangement

**Wipro NLTH**
- Verbal English: 18 questions
- Analytical: 14 questions
- Quantitative: 16 questions
- Written Communication (essay)
- Coding: 2 problems

**Capgemini**
- Game-based assessment (not traditional MCQ)
- Pseudo-code test
- Behavioural test
- Essay writing

**Accenture**
- Cognitive assessment (50 questions, 90 minutes)
- Communication assessment (English)
- Coding: 2 problems (easy to medium)
